Hi there

My name is Brian Wray. I'm a full-time staff member of the Field Museum of Natural History in Chicago, and a full-time Master's student studying Computer Science at DePaul's CDM school.

I'm interested broadly in bioinformatics, software engineering and data science. Right now I work with Field Museum curators to use DNA data in order to help further understand the nature of relationships between organisms at the species level. I work with Associate Curators Corrie Moreau (ants), Rick Ree (flowering plants), Thorsten Lumbsch (lichens), Shannon Hackett (birds), and John Bates (birds).

In addition to working with data, I spend a fair amount of time training visiting scientists in lab and computational techniques. I'm also involved in our exhibit's "Talk to the Scientist Hour", weekdays from 11-12.

Like all humans, I contain multitudes! Too much to flesh out on a simple "about" section of a scrappy little website. Hit me up and let's talk.
